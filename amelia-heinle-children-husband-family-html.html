Amelia Heinle's Children To Face Strain! Husband &amp; Divorce - Family Falling Apart

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, September 5, 2024

American actress Amelia Heinle met both of her ex-husbands, who were her co-stars on the set of two different series. However, both of her marriages ended in divorce.

The two-time Emmy winner gained popularity from the character of Stephanie Brewster on the soap opera Loving.

She has been portraying the role of Victoria Newman in the award-winning television soap opera, The Young and the Restless, since 2005.

For the regular role in the long-running series, she has won the Daytime Emmy Award two times in the category of Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 2014 and 2015, respectively.

Amelia Heinle's Bio

The industrious actress was born Amelia March Heinle on March 17, 1973, in Phoenix, Arizona. An avid traveler, Amelia has a lot of interests in dogs and possesses three pet dogs; Walter, Rufus, and Po Po.

Her Career In Succession; Loving, Jack & Jill, The Young and the Restless

Amelia started her career at the age of 20 in 1993, when she was cast as Stephanie "Steffi" Brewster in Loving.

She appeared on the show till 1995 before starring again in the show's spinoff named The City from November 1995 to January 1996.

For her alluring performance as Stephaine, Amelia was nominated for the Soap Opera Digest Award for Outstanding Female Newcomer in 1994.

In the next ten years, Amelia did many television films, including the 1997 horror TV film Quicksilver Highway, where she portrayed the role of 'Darlene.'

She appeared in many movies; 'Black Cat Run' in 1998, the western fantasy film 'Purgatory' in 1999, The Only Living Boy in New York in 2000, and the most loved, fictional television film Earth vs. the Spider in 2001.

During that period, she also did three films, At Sachem Farm, Liar's Poker, and The Limey featuring mostly in supporting roles.

Moreover, she has appeared in various television roles that include The Limey (1999), Earth vs. the Spider (2001), Another Night (2003), and CSI: Miami (2009).
