Inside RHONY star Sonja Morgans $1.6M NYC pad featuring pink walls and stunning city views Th

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, August 8, 2024

SONJA Morgan is living pretty in pink inside her feminine New York apartment that’s across the street from Central Park.

The Real Housewives of New York star, 56, traded her 4,650-square-foot townhouse for the 1,050-square-foot two-bedroom Manhattan high rise.

The girly crib - which is estimated to be worth over $1.6 million - is decked out with pastel pink walls throughout the living spaces.

She proudly showed off her kitchen which features modern white cabinets with silver handles that match the stainless-steel appliances.

The reality star can eat breakfast on the massive island she completed with sleek white chairs.

“I love my new kitchen! It’s shiny and clean and petite, manageable and modern,” she gushed.

The luxurious cooking space oversees the living room that has a white-mantled indoor fireplace along with a couch the Bravo star described as “classic and clean.”

The mother-of-one boasted about the furry addition: “And this beautiful, beautiful rug is from Nicole Miller. Very modern and fresh.”

A small table and a pair of chairs sits next to the window that leads out to the patio that features stunning views of Columbus Circle.

As the reality star stood on the terrace, she expressed: “I love the balcony. As you know, at the townhouse, I had a beautiful garden, and here, I have a slice of life from home.

“I have my plants, my herbs, my strawberries, mint, basil, everything I need to cook at home.”

Central Park is directly across the street from the outspoken star’s building.

The master bedroom is a noticeable downsize from her previous home where her room took up an entire floor.

However, the space - which is painted blue to “wards off evil spirits” - still features a large closet and its own bathroom.

Sonja said goodbye to her five bedroom, five and a half bath townhouse in 2018.

According to Bravo, she originally listed the sprawling pad for $10.75 million before she decided to rent it out.

After she initially listed the estate on the market, she found a renter to pay the massive price tag of $32,000 a month for the NYC home.

The Manhattan pad has five bedrooms and six bathrooms and came equipped with a private elevator.

Fans have seen her enviable outdoor terrace become a filming staple both on the show and the TV personality’s Instagram.

During a Real Housewives reunion special in 2018, Sonja admitted of her iconic house: “The moment no one thought would come - including myself - is finally here. I’m leaving the townhouse.

“I found a great apartment, it’s so my style. I have a fireplace and a little balcony. It’s not too high rise, because I don’t like high rises. I’m only on the 12th floor.”
