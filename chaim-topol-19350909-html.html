Chaim Topol, also spelled Haym Topol, known individually as Topol, is an actor, singer, comedian, voice actor, film producer, author and illustrator, painter from Israel. Known for his role as Tevye the Dairyman, the lead role in the musical Fiddler on the Roof, both on stage and on screen, he has performed the role over 3,500 times on shows and has enjoyed a renaissance ever since. the late 1960s. to 2009. Topol began his acting career in the service of the Israeli army on the Nahal dispatcher, then toured in Israel with the kibbutz theater and satirical companies. He is a co-founder of Haifa Theater. His groundbreaking film role came in 1964 as the protagonist in Israeli writer Ephraim Kishon's Sallah Shabati, for which he won the Golden Globe for Best New Actor - Male. Topol has appeared in more than 30 films in Israel and the United States, including Galileo, Flash Gordon and For Your Eyes Only.
