5 Best Nursing Chairs 2021 | The Sun UK

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Tuesday, July 2, 2024

NURSING CHAIRS may feel like more of an indulgence than a necessity, but whether you're breastfeeding or not, it's helpful to have a comfy chair in baby's room for an adult to relax in.

The best nursing chairs rock and are especially handy since the rocking motion can also help soothe baby to sleep. If you're looking for a chair baby can sit and rock in, don't miss our roundup of best baby bouncers.

These can morph into reading chairs for you and your little one to snuggle up in for bedtime stories, or even turn into your favourite piece of living room furniture.

This list of best nursing chairs is compiled based on the top-rated styles, according to parents.

Breastfeeding mums are always on the lookout for chairs and pillows to help them stay comfortable in the early days of near-constant baby feeding.

However, having a dedicated space for a parent to sit is just as essential if you want to give your baby a bottle or dad wants to read little one a bedtime story.

A nursing chair isn’t just for the early days; the best chairs will become favourites for the first few years and are also handy when grandparents come for a visit.

The good news is, you can easily find a baby rocking chair to suit your style, your space and your budget.

In fact, some of the best nursing chairs out there aren’t even technically designed for the nursery (we’re looking at you, Ikea Poäng chair).

Rocking chairs, glider chairs, reclining chairs... here are the best nursing chairs that will have you singing Rock A Bye Baby to your little one.

What is the point of a rocking chair?

Rocking motions and movements help soothe babies and send them off to sleep.

It's why babies tend to fall asleep in buggies and when driven around in the car.

A piece of furniture with that same rocking capability - whether a baby rocker that baby can sit in or a nursing chair for adults to relax in, can be a very useful piece of kit to invest in, especially for baby's first year.

A rocking nursing chair is a multi-tasking object: it's comfortable for feeding baby, whether they're being breastfed or bottle-fed, and the rocking motion can help calm baby when they need it.

As baby gets older a nursing chair becomes a reading chair, a relaxing chair, and possibly even a spaceship for a child's imaginary play games. The possibilities are endless!

Are rocking chairs safe for newborns?

Since parents sit in nursing rocking chairs with baby snuggled in their arms, these can be used from birth.

Most baby bouncers and rockers that you place baby in directly are also suitable for newborns - but we'd advise checking the specific rocking chair to make sure.

What makes a good nursing chair?

There are different factors to consider when choosing which nursing chair is best for you, your home and your family.

We'd recommend choosing a rocking chair which has the added benefit of movement - which is helpful for babies but also turns into a fun piece of furniture in kids' games as they get older.

For breastfeeding parents, a nursing chair with padded armrests and a padded back, like the Kub Haywood Nursing Chair, ticks every box.

It also reclines for those nights you spend sleeping next to baby's bed.

If you've got other kids running around and need an easy-clean chair, a wipe-clean option like the IKEA Poäng can be a handy addition to the home.

For longevity, pick a stylish designer chair that doubles as a nursing chair - it will feel like an investment piece in any room of the house, but will also get you through those first few months of sleeplessness.
