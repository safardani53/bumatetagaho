Babestation models jet into Irish town to issue grovelling apology to locals after sex line blunder

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, July 18, 2024

THREE Babestation workers jetted into Westport today — to issue a grovelling apology to punters.

The ladies from the UK phone sex-line channel made good on their promise to visit locals in the Co Mayo tourist hot-spot.

For months now unsuspecting residents have been receiving calls from horny Irish men who mix up the dialling codes and think they are getting through to Babestation.

The lusty late-night lads are after a pow-wow with babes like gorgeous London-based ladies Vicky Narni, Priya Young and Alexa Brooke.

But instead the dozy diallers are causing distress to elderly people in the west coast town.

Since the story broke Babestation have taken action and changed the number.

So the calls will now stop and Babestation callers will be just a bad memory for those affected.

A turbulent landing at Knock Airport, a cancelled hire car and gale force conditions made for a rough start for the models.

But they kept their best side out when they finally arrived in Westport for a photo op — over an hour later than scheduled.
Weather conditions meant the girls were greeted by a dozen journalists and only a handful of locals.
But what the crowd lacked in number it made up for in enthusiasm.
As a large group of journalists and photographers waited for the models — originally due to arrive at 1.30pm — to get there, a traffic ­warden was able to give them an update.
To widespread laughter he joked: “They’ll be here at 4pm lads, I was talking to one of them last night at 5.15 in the morning.” Several local men jumped in for selfies with Vicky, Priya and Alexa but each refused to give their name when asked.

One said: “No way, I”m a married man.”Stopping to assess the picture quality, while the wind launched his jacket over his head, he smiled and quipped. “Pure class. The lads are going to love it.”

And off he went back over the bridge with a spring in his step.

Another young man said he was delighted to see the girls in town.

He said: “Sure it’s only a bit of craic. These girls certainly didn’t set out to hurt anyone. It’s the stupid, sweaty moron at the end of the phone who is causing all the hassle.

“Jokes aside it was a horrible experience for the people it happened to. Who wants to get calls in the middle of the night from some randy idiot in Dublin?”

When told one of the people affected works for the emergency services and had no choice but to leave his phone on the girls seemed genuinely upset.

Vicky said: “That’s awful. We didn’t know that. As you can see we are fun girls but we know there is a very serious undertone to this.

"It is not funny at all if you are getting calls from 10.30 at night to all hours of the morning. It was an error.

"Not our error but we are doing everything we can to make it right.

As you can see we are fun girls but we know there is a very serious undertone to this. It is not funny at all if you are getting calls from 10.30 at night to all hours of the morning.

“Truly the last thing we want to do is upset anyone.

"The media frenzy around it is one thing but we realise how upset people are and that’s why we wanted to come over.”

Alexa Brooke reached out to Minister for Rural Affairs Michael Ring who declined to meet them — after previously saying his constituents in Mayo were “aggravated and annoyed”.

She said: “We just want to say we are sorry. We would have loved to have met him.

“It would have been lovely but we understand he is probably a very busy man.”
