Grady Sizemore | From Beginning To Retirement

Since his early days, Grady was highly determined to make his life in sports. Mostly, he was fond of baseball, basketball, and football.

Eventually, the USA-born initiated his professional career as a baseball player in 2004. He played in the field by representing the MLB’s team, Cleveland Indians.

Sizemore contributed to Cleveland as one of the finest players from 2004 to 2011.

Moreover, Grady represented other teams like Boston Red Sox (2014), Philadelphia Phillies (2014-2015), and Tampa Bay Rays (2015).

Overall, Sizemore secured a record with a batting average of 265, 150 homer runs, and runs batted in 518.

Honors and Awards

Grady was a three-time All-Star from 2006 to 2008. Also, he won Gold Glove Award two times (2007 and 2008). Plus, he received the Silver Slugger Award in 2008.

Retirement

While Grady was on his journey of being one of the greatest baseballers, his health didn’t cooperate with him.

During his professional career, Sizemore had to go through many minor surgeries. For instance, in 2009, he underwent elbow and lower abdomen surgery. 

Again, between 2010 to 2013, Sizemore had to endure surgeries on his left knee, right knee, and back.

Due to such unavoidable incidents, Grady bid farewell to his athletic expedition in 2015.
