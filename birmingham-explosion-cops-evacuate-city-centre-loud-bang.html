Birmingham 'explosion' - cops evacuate part of city centre amid reports of 'loud bang' in theatre

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, May 24, 2024

HUNDREDS of theatre-goers were evacuated tonight amid reports of a loud "explosion" in Birmingham city centre.

Parts of the city were on lockdown as Repertory Theatre in Broad Street was emptied following a "loud bang" in the area.

Initial reports suggest the bang was caused by a technical fault after all three emergency services scrambled to the scene.

In a Tweet written shortly before 9pm, West Midlands Police wrote: "All emergency services are currently at Birmingham's Repertory Theatre, Broad St following reports of an explosion.

"Initial indications are that this was a technical fault in a staff only area.

"No casualties are reported. Everyone has been evacuated are are being kept safe."

Earlier this evening residents shared reports of a "weird explosion noise" in the city centre.

Chris Jennings asked: "Anyone just hear that ridiculously loud bang around the #mailbox in #Birmingham"?

Twitter user blank added: "Weird explosion noise in birmingham city centre just by the Barclay card arena."

West Midlands Police sought to reassure residents with further updates, writing on Twitter: "Initial indications are that this was a technical fault.

"No casualties are reported. There is a large presence by all emergency services."

Birmingham Repertory Theatre, known normally as The Rep, seats around 1,000 and was tonight scheduled to stage Brief Encounter.
