Best cheap suitcases 2023: Including IT Luggage, Tripp, Featherstone and more

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 2, 2024

THE best cheap suitcases offer many of the same functions and features as their more expensive counterparts but don’t require the same level of investment.

Whether you’re a fan of soft or hard shell luggage, you can find cases to suit your budget in all popular sizes – cabin, medium and large. And you don’t have to settle for boring black either as there are loads of fun colours out there to put you in the holiday mood before you even board the plane.

Some budget suitcases have integral locks, but if they don’t then it’s generally an easy process to fit a small padlock.

Inside, they often have interior pockets to keep small items and jewellery separate, as well as dividers and the usual luggage straps.

Telescopic handles and spinner wheels mean even cheap suitcases are supremely manoeuvrable across all types of floors and you may find soft cases even have pockets on the outside for last-minute airport buys.

When they’re this good, why spend more on luggage? The money you save will go towards that stunning holiday outfit or your spending money while you’re away.

What is the cabin suitcase limit?

To avoid getting caught out and made to pay pricey fees, it's crucial to check out the airline's cabin luggage restrictions before you get to the airport.

Each airline has its own specific set of rules, and they can vary widely.

On international flights, the cabin bag size is usually around 56 x 45 x 25cm.

However, budget airlines generally have tighter restrictions, with sizes dependent on your ticket class.

Ryanair recently updated its cabin bag policy, so check it out here.

You can also find easyJet cabin bags policy here, as well as those for British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, Emirates and Jet2.com.

How to measure a suitcase

Most manufacturers state the measurements of their suitcases online.

But if it’s an old case or an unusual shape, you might need to measure it yourself.

To start, place your suitcase in the upright position on a flat surface with any extendable handles tucked in.

Then measure from the flat surface to the top of the case (usually the handle) - this will give you the height of the case and it should include the wheels.

Then you just turn it on its side and repeat to get the width, and then lie it on its back to get the depth.

In all of the measurements, make sure that you include any handles and wheels as airlines count these towards the size of your bag.
