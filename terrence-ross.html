Terrence Ross | Early Life & Family

Terrence James Elijah Ross, popularly known as Terrence Ross was born in Portland, Oregon, on February 5, 1991. He is one of the three children of Terry Ross and Marcine Parker.

His father, Terry, had played men’s basketball in Continental Basketball Association and yanked the slam-dunk designation with the Tri-City in 1995. His mother, Marcine Ross, also played basketball in her early days.

Born and brought up in an athletic lineage, it is understandable that Ross inherited his athletic interest in sports from his parents.

He played basketball from any early age and is now known as one of the most prominent basketball players. 

Ross was equally proficient in his studies. He scored a 3.7 GPA in his college. 

A sudden catastrophe came to Ross’s life when his parents divorced, and he started living thoroughly with his mother. 

Terrence Ross | Basketball Career

High School Career

Ross enrolled at Jefferson High School in Portland, Oregon, during his first and second years.

In the meantime, he helped lead Jefferson to the first three consecutive state championships, which won the Oregon 5A Player of the Year.

During his junior year in 2008-2009, he went to Montrose Christian School in Rockville, Maryland. Then, he averaged 13.5 points per game and stood as the first-team All-Metro.

He was regarded as a four-star recruit by ESPN.com; he was enumerated as the No.5 small forward and No. 30 player in 2010.

Moreover, he committed to playing basketball for the University of Washington. 

College Career

Ross attended Washington University after finishing high school. He recorded 8.0 points, 2.8 rebounds, and 1.0 assists in about 34 games during his freshman season with the Huskies.

He logged 15.3 points and 2.7 rebounds per game in the 2011 Pac-10 Tournament and was named to the Pac-10 All-Tournament team.

Furthermore, Ross was named first-team All-Pac-12 honors during his sophomore season. He helped Washington enter the 2012 National Invitation Tournament semi-finals with the most acceptable score of 25 points and added 5.5 rebounds per game.

Professional Career

Toronto Raptors

Terrence Ross was selected as the eighth overall pick by the Toronto Raptors in the 2012 NBA draft. He signed his rookie contract with the Toronto Raptors in July 2012.

Ross overthrew Jeremy Evans in the 2013 Sprite Slam Dunk Contest in February 2013.

He logged a career-high and franchise-tying 51 points against the Los Angeles Clippers in January 2014. Ross became the first player in NBA history to score 50 points, averaging about 10 points per game. 

Similarly, he recorded a new season-high, 27 points over the Detroit Pistons in February 2016. He recorded his first double-double with 24 points and added ten rebounds against the Brooklyn Nets.

Also, he recorded a career playoff-high with 19 points in the conference semi-final against the Miami Heat.

In December 2016, Ross logged a new season-high 25 points in a 122-100 victory against the Milwaukee Bucks.

Orlando Magic

Terrence Ross was traded to Orlando Magic in February 2017.

Ross recorded game-high 24 points in a victory against the Atlanta Hawks. Similarly, in November 2017, he scored a season-high 22 points against Minnesota Timberwolves.

Then, in February 2019, he logged 32 points and set six 3-pointers in a victory over the Timberwolves. Ross became the first player to score 200 three points during the 2018-19 regular season.

He ended his 2020-21 season averaging 15.6 points, 3.4 rebounds, and 2.3 assists. The following season, 2021-22, Ross played 63 games for Orlando Magic and recorded an average of 10 points, 2.6 rebounds, 1.8 assists, and 0.2 blocks.

As of this November, 2023, in the 2022-23 season, he has made 36 appearances averaging 8.3 points, 2.1 rebounds, 1.4 assists, and 0.2 blocks.
