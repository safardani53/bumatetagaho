Man killed by a single punch after confronting teens lobbing fireworks in the street

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, May 26, 2024

A MAN was killed by a single punch - after he challenged youths believed to be throwing fireworks in the street.

The 59-year-old who has not yet been named, was outside Dixy Chicken takeaway on Reed Hill, off Yorkshire Street, at around 9.15pm on Thursday evening when he was assaulted.

He fell backwards and hit his head on the floor, suffering a serious head injury, before he was taken to hospital.

The victim was having a cigarette outside the Reed Hotel pub, when he told the teens to stop throwing fireworks, Manchester Evening News reports.

He then walked to the takeaway, before he was approached by around five men.

A witness told the news site that the argument unfolded next to the door of the takeaway.

But after the man collapsed, the group ran off towards the direction of Saint Mary's Gate.

Cops have launched an investigation into the attack to establish how he the night's events unfolded.

An 18-year-old man who was arrested on suspicion on section 18 assault, has reportedly since been bailed.

Detective Inspector Wes Knights said: "Our thoughts are with the man’s family at this tragic time, we are doing all that we can to find those responsible and give the family the answers they deserve.

“I need to hear from anyone who was in the Reed Hill area who may have seen something.

“It’s a busy part of town in the evening and I would urge people to come forward with information which could be vital for us trying to establish how this man came to be seriously assaulted.”

Anyone with information is asked to call police on 101, quoting incident number 2356 of September 28, or Crimestoppers, anonymously, on 0800 555 111.
