Sean McVay | Father, Early Life And Education

Sean McVay, the youngest head coach in NFL history, was born in the city of Dayton, Ohio, USA.

He was born to his father, Tim McVay, and mother, Cindy McVay. Likewise, his father, Sean, was a football player and played as a defensive back at Indiana University.

Not to mention, even his grandfather, John McVay, was a head coach at the University of Dayton from 1965 to 1972.

Later in the 1970s, he served as the general manager for the New York Giants and San Francisco 49ers during the team’s five Super Bowl championships during the 1980 and 1990s. 

Similarly, McVay’s family lived in Dayton till Sean was six years old and moved to Georgia. There he graduated from Marist School, located in Brookhaven, in 2004. 

Moving on, McVay attended Miami University in Oxford, Ohio, where he continued playing football as a wide receiver.

Sean graduated from the University in 2009 with a B.S. degree in Health and Sports Studies. Also, he is an American citizen who belongs to the white ethnicity.

Sean McVay | Career

Football Career

Before he started working as a coach, Sean himself was a football player. He comes from a family where his father and grandfather were also part of the football team.

McVay started as a quarterback and defensive back for the War Eagles high school football team during high school. Likewise, the football star made history by amassing 1000 yards rushing and passing in consecutive seasons.

Moreover, he totaled 2,600 yards rushing and 40 rushing touchdowns during his career and led the team to a win. In his senior year, Sean’s name got included as the Georgia 4A Offensive Player of the Year. 

After that, he enrolled at Miami University in Oxford. There he played college football as a wide receiver for three years, from 2004 to 2007. He even earned Miami’s Scholar-Athlete Award back in 2007.

Likewise, his college football career ended with 39 receptions for 312 yards for the Red Hawks.

Coaching Career

Tampa Bay Buccaneers & Florida Tuskers

After graduating from college, someone of his caliber would have gone professional; instead, Sean worked as a coach.

In 2008, he became an assistant wide receivers coach for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers under the guidance of head coach Jon Gruden.

Likewise, the following year, McVay became the quality control/wide receivers coach for the Florida Tuskers of the United Football League(UFL).

Washington Redskins

In 2010, Sean became the offensive assistant coach for the Washington Redskins under head coach Mike Shanahan.

Moreover, in 2011, McVay was promoted to tight ends coach, a position he held till the 2013 season. The new coach was also promoted to offensive coordinator by new Redskins head coach Jay Gruden.

Under his coaching, the team made some significant process. He changed the team’s offense into the 12th-ranked pass offense.

Sean also worked with the club until the 2016 season, while the team finished third overall in total yards and 11th in points with 403.4 total yards and 24.8 points per game.

Los Angeles Rams

Good things were indeed on their way for Sean. At the age of 30, Sean McVay became the Los Angeles Rams’ 28th head coach, making him the youngest head coach in NFL history.

The same year, Matt LaFleur filled the offensive coordinator role, with whom Sean had worked before in Washington.

Amazingly, the Rams finished with a top-scoring offense in the league that year after having the lowest score the previous year.

Thanks to his contribution, Sean was even named Coach of the Year by the Pro Football Writers of America in January 2018. 

Also, he led his team, Los Angeles Rams, to their first title in 22 years. The Rams defeated the Bengals 23-20 in the Super Bowl on February 13, 2022.
