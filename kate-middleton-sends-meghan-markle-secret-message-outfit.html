Kate Middleton sends Meghan Markle a secret message with her Christmas carol service outfit, accordi

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, March 14, 2024

THE Princess of Wales has invited Brits to tune into her "special Christmas carol service" - and “sent a resounding message” with her outfit choice.

The festive teaser clip, which was released last night, came in the wake of royal author Omid Scobie’s bombshell Endgame book.

Early Dutch copies of Scobie's poison-filled Endgame suggested Kate and Charles were the two Royals who had made remarks about the skin colour of Harry and Meghan's first child, Archie.

The pair were allegedly accused by Meghan in a letter to King.

In the English versions, Scobie says UK laws prevent him from naming names.

A teaser clip for the Royal Carols: Together At Christmas programme featuring Kate aired on ITV1 at 9.45pm on Thursday.

More on the royals

Kate looked angelic wearing a white Self Portrait jacket with a pearl trim and sequin embellishments.

And according to fashion editor Amber Graafland, the colour choice sent a strong message.

Speaking to Fabulous, Amber claimed: “Regardless of what she’s feeling after being named as one of the royals accused of making racist comments, Kate's chic boucle Christmas Carol Concert outfit allowed her to convey a thousand words. 

“Well, the colour to be more precise.

Most read in Royals

“White symbolises purity, innocence and neutrality. 

“It also represents new beginnings, and a clean slate, helping us through times of stress and allowing us to put the past behind us whilst preparing to move on.

“So, regardless of what the Princess is really feeling, the message was resoundingly loud and clear. 

“Now let that be the end of the matter.”

Amber said that the opportunity for senior royal family members to articulate their personal views is “pretty much non-existent”.

She continued: “However, this inability to ever express an opinion or defend oneself does explain why certain members of the royal family have become rather adept at conveying subtle messages through their clothing.

“The Princess of Wales being a perfect case in point.”

The Together at Christmas service, hosted by Kate, is due to take place at the Gothic Westminster Abbey in central London on Friday, and will be broadcast on Christmas Eve at 7.45pm.

In the teaser, Kate says: "Join me this Christmas Eve for a special carol service, as we say a heartfelt thank-you to all those supporting the very youngest members of our society during those crucial early years."

The service, supported by The Royal Foundation, will honour those who work to support babies, young children and families in communities across the UK.

Linked to Kate's Shaping Us campaign, the service will showcase "the hope of new life and why it is so important to build supportive, nurturing worlds around children and the adults in their lives".

The Westminster Abbey choir will perform popular carols, alongside musical performances by the likes of Jacob Collier, Freya Ridings and James Bay, and a special duet by Beverley Knight and Adam Lambert.

Readings will be given by speakers including the Prince of Wales, Micheal Ward, Emma Willis, Roman Kemp, and Jim Broadbent, while a specially commissioned poem written by Joseph Coelho, the Children's Laureate, will be read by Leonie Elliott.
