Harry Styles reveals songs 'about exes Caroline Flack and Taylor Swift' as he tweets tracklist to hi

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, May 22, 2024

HARRY Styles has revealed the track list for his debut solo album - and fans are already speculating about the meaning behind the tracks.

Twitter users have speculated one track called ‘Carolina’ could be an ode to former lover, Caroline Flack and Ever Since New York is rumoured to be related to the time Harry spent in New York with ex-girlfriend, Taylor Swift.

Harry’s confirmed that the self-titled debut will be released on May 12 on his own record label, Erskine Records.

The 10 track album has been produced by the people behind hits for Bruno Mars, Kanye West, Beyonce and Taylor Swift.

However one fan has tweeted their anger to the 23-year-old singer, saying: “Do you think I waited a year and half for ten tracks? Next!”

Many fans have been quick to assume that Carolina is reference to Harry’s brief relationship with TV presenter, Caroline Flack.

One fan said: “Carolina? Caroline Flack I’m calling it, they’re still in love.”

Another added: “The fact that there’s a song called “Carolina” makes me think he hasn’t got over Caroline Flack but… who knows?”

Others have been quick to assume that Ever Since New York is a reference to Taylor Swift who also had a track referencing the Big Apple – Welcome to New York – on her last album, 1989.

During their relationship Harry and Taylor spent time in New York and fans are running wild with speculation.

One social media user wrote: “Harry Styles has a song on his album called Since New York. Taylor Swift lives in New York. See where I’m going with this?”

Harry has also announced the album will be released in several limited physical formats including a CD package with 32 page booklet featuring photos taken during the making of the album and a white vinyl LP.
