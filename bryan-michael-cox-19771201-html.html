Bryan-Michael Cox (Music Producer) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, September 26, 2024

Bryan-Michael Paul Cox is an American record producer and musician who is best known for many of his works with a number of platinum artists, including Usher, Mariah Carey, Mary J. Blige and Toni. Braxton. Among his most notable works is "Be Without You" for Mary J. Blige, "Burn", "Confessions Part II" and "U Got It Bad" for Usher, and "Shake It Off", "I Stay In Love" and "You Don't Know What To Do" for Mariah Carey. Cox also set a Guinness World Record for the longest consecutive period of success on the chart, breaking records previously held by the Beatles. After growing up in Houston, Texas, Cox moved to Atlanta to pursue his professional music career and soon established a close working relationship with regular production partner Jermaine Dupri. Over the course of his career, Cox has been responsible for over 100 million album sales, 35 No.1 hits and 12 Grammy nominations.
