Inside Rolling Stones rocker Keith Richards 80th birthday plans as the band celebrate 11th No1 al

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, April 24, 2024

IT isn’t only his latest No1 album that Rolling Stones guitarist Keith Richards has to celebrate – but an upcoming 80th birthday in December as well.

Bandmate Sir Mick Jagger threw a no-expense-spared A-list party to mark the same milestone in July.

But Keith is planning something even wilder, by jetting off on a safari holiday.

I’m told he is whisking his extended family away on the getaway in Africa.

A source said: “Keith isn’t afraid of wild beasts and loves seeing them roaming in the wild.

“It’s been a dream to do a big holiday with all his family at a luxury resort in Africa and have that once-in-a-lifetime kind of experience all together.

"He turns the big 8-0 in December and he’s hoping to get out before then and live it up in the sunshine and then spend Christmas with his family.”

It definitely beats the bleak weather back in Blighty.

The Stones, who also include guitarist Ronnie Wood, shifted 72,000 copies of their latest album Hackney Diamonds in the past week, making it the third fastest-selling release of the year behind records from Lewis Capaldi and Ed Sheeran.

That should give Keef plenty of cash to splash on his dream holiday.

Kendall & Hailey's blackout

KYLIE Jenner got her sister Kendall and pal Hailey Bieber to model outfits from her new clothing label.

Arnie's got hay-lister pets

SCREEN action man Arnold Schwarzenegger is wanted by Hollywood bosses – but this time it’s because of his pet animals.

The Terminator actor’s pony Whiskey and donkey Lulu are in high demand.

Promoting his self-help book, Be Useful: Seven Tools for Life, at London’s Royal Albert Hall this week, Arnie said: “The videos I did with them in lockdown went viral, they were so popular.

"My horse and donkey were offered movie roles afterwards. They wanted me to be in films with them. I couldn’t believe it, but that’s Hollywood.”

If it wasn’t for Arnold’s girlfriend Heather Milligan, Lulu and Whiskey may not have got so famous.

It was she who suggested Whiskey was lonely and needed a pal.
