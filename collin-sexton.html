Collin Sexton | Early Life, Family, and Education

Collin Darnell Sexton was born on January 4, 1999. His parents, Darnell Sexton and Gia Sexton raised him and his siblings in Marietta, Georgia.

His father is a pharmaceutical salesman and a former coach, while his mother Gia is a nurse. In addition, he has two siblings, one older brother, Jordan Sexton, and one older sister, Giauna Sexton.

Furthermore, Collin started playing basketball when he was just three years old. He used to play with his brother, Jordan, in their garage until late at night.

At that time, Jordan often won, and Collin refused to end the game in a loss.

Even though his sister did not play basketball, it was her idea to put up a ring in their garage so that her youngest brother could play. 

She was and still is a very supportive sister. Furthermore, Sexton attended Hillgrove High School in Powder Springs, Georgia.

Later, he transferred to Pebblebrook High School in Mableton, Georgia, where he played for the Falcons. After that, he attended the University of Alabama and played for the Alabama Crimson Tide.

Collin Sexton | High School Career

After transferring to Pebblebrook High School, Collin played with the Falcons and led them to a 2016 Georgia Region 3-6A title.

Also, he helped the Falcons to the 2016 Georgia Class 6A state championship game with 23 points on average, 7.4 rebounds, and 2.9 assists.

During his senior year, ESPN and two other websites considered Sexton a five-star recruit and one of the best players in the 2017 recruiting class.

Overall, he ranked seventh in a recruit, second in point guard in the 2017 high school class. Also, Collin won the McDonald’s Slam Dunk championship while in high school.

Despite having so many options as one of the top recruits, Collin committed to the Alabama Crimson Tide and signed his Letter of Intent on the same day, i.e., on November 10, 2016.

Collin Sexton | College career

Sexton chose the University of Alabama and played for coach Avery who had played in the NBA.

In a game against the University of Minnesota, he recorded 40 points while playing shorthanded during most of the second half on November 25, 2017. 

Similarly, in the SEC Men’s Basketball Tournament opening game, Collin Sexton recorded 27 points against Texas A&M University, including the game-winning buzzer beater.

Then in the quarterfinal, he scored 31 points in a win against Auburn University. Likewise, Collin scored 21 points against Kentucky, losing the game in the semifinals.

Subsequently, Collin Sexton was nominated to the all-tournament team after recording an average of 26.3 PPG (points per game), three assists, and five rebounds in three games.

Additionally, in his one year at Alabama University, Collin had a 4.0-grade point average.

After the Alabama Crimson Tide lost in the 2018 NCAA Men’s Basketball Tournament, Sexton announced his decision to drop out of his final three seasons of collegiate eligibility and go for the 2018 NBA draft.

Collin Sexton | Professional career

Cleveland Cavaliers 

It was rumored that Collin would be a number one pick in the draft. However, Sexton was drafted as the eighth overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2018 NBA draft on July 21, 2018.

Not to mention, he was the highest-selected player from Alabama since 1995 after Antonio McDyess.

Following the draft, Sexton made his NBA Summer League debut on July 6, 2018, where he scored 15 points with seven rebounds.

Furthermore, Collin made his NBA debut appearance with the Cavaliers on October 17, 2018. Likewise, he scored nine points and three rebounds in a 104–116 defeat over the Toronto Raptors.

He also scored a career-high 29 points on November 24, 2018, against the Houston Rockets in a 117–108 victory.

Similarly, he scored a season-high 29 points on December 9, 2018, against the Washington Wizards in a victorious game.

Not to mention, Collin surpassed Kyrie Irving for three-pointers on March 8, 2019. To top that off, he also surpassed 1,000 career points in a short time.

During the second week of March 2019, Sexton had the best week of his rookie campaign with 26.0 points, 3.8 assists, and 2.5 rebounds.

Even more so, he became the first rookie to score 23 plus points in seven consecutive games between March 8 to March 22, since 1998 after Tim Duncan.

Moreover, he was the only rookie in Cleveland history to score at least 23 points successively.

Consequently, he got nominated to the Rising Stars Game at the 2020 NBA All-Star Weekend as a replacement for Tyler Herro, who was injured.

Luckily for Collin, he scored 21 points with five rebounds and three assists for Team USA.

Furthermore, the Young Bull scored a new career-high record with 41 points in a defeat against the Boston Celtics on March 4, 2020, at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse.

Under-17 World Championship

Sexton represented the United States with the 2016 USA Men’s U17 World Championship team at the 2016 FIBA Under-17 World Championship in Zaragoza, Spain.

Not to mention, he took home a gold medal and the Most Valuable Player (MVP) of the tournament award.
